### Cambridge GCSE - Grammar and Structures

#### Articles
- Definite, indefinite, partitive
- Definite article with titles and countries

#### Nouns
- Gender
- Singular and plural forms, including common and less common irregulars (e.g. la mano, il cinema, il dita, le dita)

#### Negatives
- non…ancora/mai/niente/neanche/nessuno/più/che
- alcuno, nemmeno, nulla, affatto, non solo... ma anche, né...né

#### Adjectives
- Agreement, number and gender
- Position and basic exceptions
- Common irregular adjectives before nouns (e.g. bello, buono)
- Comparative and superlative (regular and irregular)
- Demonstrative (questo, quello singular and plural)
- Indefinite (ogni, qualche, alcuni, uno, qualcuno, qualcosa, etc.)
- Possessive (with articles; exceptions for family members)
- Interrogative (che; qual/quale/quali; quanto/a, quanti/e)

#### Adverbs
- Formation and position
- Adverbs of time and place
- Use of ci (c'è, ci sono, ci vado)
- Comparative and superlative (including meglio/peggio)
- Interrogative (direct and indirect)
- Common expressions of quantity (abbastanza, mezzo, molto, poco, etc.)

#### Pronouns
- Subject (including si)
- Object (direct and indirect); position and order with infinitive, gerund and imperative
- Reflexive
- Demonstrative (questo, quello; use of ci)
- Interrogative (chi, che, che cosa, cosa, quale, quanto)
- Relative (che, il quale, cui, chi, quello che, il cui)
- Disjunctive
- Possessive
- Indefinite (nessuno, niente, ogni, qualcuno, qualcosa, etc.)
- Use of ecco with pronouns (eccomi!, eccolo!)
- Use of ne

#### Verbs
- Regular and irregular forms, including reflexive verbs
- All persons, singular and plural
- Negative forms
- Interrogative forms
- Imperative
- Modes of address (tu, voi, Lei)
- Gerund
- Impersonal (fa; bisogna + infinitive)
- Verbs followed by infinitive, with or without preposition
- Dependent infinitive (e.g. far vedere)

**Tenses:**
- Present and present continuous (stare + gerund)
- Perfect (avere or essere + past participle – including reflexive verbs and agreements)
- Imperfect and imperfect continuous
- Pluperfect
- Immediate future (stare per and use of present)
- Future
- Future perfect
- Conditional (present; perfect tense)
- Present subjunctive in commonly used forms
- Past subjunctive + conditional (e.g. se fossi ricco, comprerei…)
- Passive voice
- Perfect infinitive (e.g. after dopo)

#### Conjunctions
- Coordinating (e/ed; ma; o, o…o; oppure)
- Subordinating (perché, poiché, visto che, se, anche se, quando)

#### Number
- All ordinal and cardinal numbers

#### Time
- Date
- Time, including 24-hour clock, years
- Uses of da, fa, fra/tra with perfect and imperfect tenses

- Present tense of regular verbs (-are, -ere, -ire)
- Essential irregular verbs (essere, avere, andare, venire, fare, stare, etc.)
- Articles (definite and indefinite)
- Nouns (gender, singular and plural)
- Basic adjectives and agreement
- Subject and object pronouns
- Prepositions (a, in, di, da, con)
- Possessive adjectives
- Passato prossimo (introduction)
- Reflexive verbs
- Numbers and time

### A2 Level (Nuovo Espresso 2) - Key Grammar Points:
- Imperfect tense
- Contrast passato prossimo vs imperfect
- Conditional present
- Imperative (tu, Lei, voi forms)
- Direct and indirect pronouns (full system)
- Combined pronouns
- Relative pronouns (che, cui)
- Comparatives and superlatives
- Future simple
- Modal verbs in past tenses
- Introduction to subjunctive (present)

### B1 Level (Nuovo Espresso 3) - Key Grammar Points:
- Trapassato prossimo
- Combined pronouns (full mastery)
