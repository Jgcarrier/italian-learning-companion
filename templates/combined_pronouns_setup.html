üí° How Combined Pronouns Work:

When using both indirect and direct object pronouns together:
Order: Indirect + Direct

Key Changes:

  • mi, ti, ci, vi ‚Üí me, te, ce, ve (before lo/la/li/le)
  • gli, le ‚Üí glie (combines with lo/la/li/le into ONE WORD)

With mi/ti/ci/vi:

  • me lo = it to me (masc)
  • te la = it to you (fem)
  • ce li = them to us (masc)
  • ve le = them to you pl (fem)

Example: Mi dai il libro? ‚Üí Te lo do (I give it to you)

With gli/le (to him/her/them):

  • glielo = it to him/her/them (masc) - ONE WORD
  • gliela = it to him/her/them (fem) - ONE WORD
  • glieli = them to him/her/them (masc) - ONE WORD
  • gliele = them to him/her/them (fem) - ONE WORD

Example: Dai il regalo a Marco? ‚Üí Glielo do (I give it to him)

  • glielo/gliela/glieli/gliele work for BOTH singular (lui/lei) AND plural (loro)
  • Always attach to infinitives/gerunds: Devo darglielo (I must give it to him)
  • With imperatives: Daglielo! (Give it to him!)
