The imperative is used for commands, instructions, and requests. It has different forms depending on who you're addressing:

Tu (informal you):
  • -are verbs: parla! (speak!), mangia! (eat!)
  • -ere verbs: prendi! (take!), scrivi! (write!)
  • -ire verbs: apri! (open!), parti! (leave!)
  • Irregular: va'! (go!), fa'! (do/make!), di'! (say!), sii! (be!)
Lei (formal you):
  • -are verbs: parli! (speak!), mangi! (eat!)
  • -ere/-ire: prenda! (take!), apra! (open!)
  • Used for polite requests: Scusi! (Excuse me!), Venga! (Come!)
Noi (let's):
  • Andiamo! (Let's go!), Mangiamo! (Let's eat!)
  • Same as present tense noi form
Voi (you plural):
  • Parlate! (Speak!), Venite! (Come!), State attenti! (Pay attention!)
  • Same as present tense voi form

Note: Negative commands for tu use "non + infinitive": Non parlare! (Don't speak!)
