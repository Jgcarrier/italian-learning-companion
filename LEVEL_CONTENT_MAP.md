## ğŸ¯ Design Philosophy by Level

### A1 - Beginner
**Focus:** Building foundation
- Basic verb tenses (present, passato prossimo)
- Essential vocabulary
- Simple time expressions
- Foundational grammar

**Why these choices:**
- Passato Prossimo is essential for beginners
- Auxiliary choice (avere vs essere) is fundamental
- Limited grammar to avoid overwhelm
- Core vocabulary building

### A2 - Elementary
**Focus:** Expanding capabilities
- All verb tenses for everyday conversation
- More complex grammar (prepositions, negations)
- Broader vocabulary
- Full practice suite

**Why these choices:**
- Introduces imperfetto (past habits/descriptions)
- Adds futuro (talking about plans)
- Reflexive verbs (common in daily Italian)
- More sophisticated grammar structures

### B1/B2 - Intermediate/Upper
**Focus:** Fluency and sophistication
- Removes basic passato prossimo (assumed knowledge)
- Focuses on nuanced verb usage
- Advanced vocabulary
- Complex grammar combinations

**Why these choices:**
- Passato Prossimo basics assumed mastered
- Emphasizes imperfetto (more subtle)
- Prepares for complex conversations
- Grammar at sentence level, not just word level

### GCSE - Exam Prep
**Focus:** UK curriculum alignment
- Exam-relevant verb tenses
- Common exam topics
- Grammar tested in exams
- Vocabulary for exam scenarios

**Why these choices:**
- Mirrors GCSE Italian syllabus
- Covers all required tenses
- Exam-style questions
- Practical for test preparation

## ğŸ“ Notes

**Level Filtering Logic:**
- A1: Basics only (passato prossimo, essential grammar)
- A2: Full feature set (all 17 types)
- B1+: Removes basics, keeps advanced content

**Content Difficulty:**
- Same practice type gets harder at higher levels
- A1 vocab: casa (house), cane (dog)
- B2 vocab: sostenibile (sustainable), compromesso (compromise)

**Why Remove Passato Prossimo at B1+:**
- Assumed mastered at A2
- B1+ students need to practice more nuanced tenses
- Keeps menu focused and not overwhelming
- Students can drop to A2 if they need review
